The Detective in Dr. Watson's Words

When Dr. Watson first met Mr. Holmes at St. Bart's Hospital, he was unaware of the man's true profession. Based on their observations of Holmes's habits and knowledge, Watson compiled the following list of activities and interests before Holmes revealed to him his profession as a consulting detective:

  • Literature: Knowledge appears nil.
  • Philosophy: Knowledge appears nil.
  • Astronomy: Knowledge appears nil.
  • Politics: Knowledge appears feeble.
  • Botany: Variable. Profound in knowledge of belladonna, opium, and poisons generally; knows nothing of practical gardening.
  • Geology: Practical but limited. Can distinguish different soils at a glance. After walks, shows splashes upon his trousers, which can tell him their position in London.
  • Chemistry: Profound.
  • Anatomy: Accurate, but unsystematic.
  • Sensational Literature: Immense. He seems to know all details of every horror perpetrated in the century.
  • Violin-playing: Skilled player. Plays strange, sentimental pieces, or occasionally rapid compositions of his own.
  • Gymnastics: Excellent stick-man, boxer, and swordsman.
  • Documents: Great knowledge of British law.

(Source: Arthur Conan Doyle, "A Study in Scarlet", Chapter 2)
